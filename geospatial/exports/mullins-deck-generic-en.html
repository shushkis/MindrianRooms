The Strongest Domain and the One We Resolved

Domain 1 and Domain 6

Domain 1 -- Irreversible Pain (5/5)

When a parcel defaults away, the claimant loses everything, permanently. A system that changes that outcome -- even probabilistically -- is worth almost any price.

Domain 6 -- Historical-Script OCR (Upgraded to 4/5)

Flagged YELLOW at first. Existing models already hit 88-97% accuracy, and LLM-based correction gets to a 1.16% error rate. A 2-3 month engineering sprint -- not an open research problem.

Due Diligence Never Really Ends

Domain 3 -- Re-checked

Two adjacent-market players surfaced after the initial analysis -- a reminder that a Mullins scorecard is a snapshot, not a verdict.

Player A -- Established Government/Municipal GIS

Ongoing registry, permitting, and taxation for local authorities. No historical aerial imagery, no legacy-script documents, no focus on unregistered land. Not a capability threat -- a distribution-speed watch item only.

Player B -- Manual Expert Aerial-Photo Interpretation

Serves law firms and private disputes over land that is already registered. Capability overlap, not customer overlap -- different audience, different land status.

Conclusion: no direct rival in the specific niche. Domain 3 stays green, flagged for re-check before the next decision gate.
