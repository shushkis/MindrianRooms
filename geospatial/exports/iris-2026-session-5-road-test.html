HaMuzim — Ground Truth

How can we help the government bodies who bear the cost of unresolved land disputes — the property custodian (רמ"י), for whom every unproven private claim defaults to state ownership, and the Civil Administration's סגן רמ"א, who owns the resolution backlog — do evaluate competing ownership claims against an independent historical spatial record under conditions where boundary markers have vanished, evidence is scattered across decades of heterogeneous sources, and any output must survive adversarial cross-examination in order to issue legally defensible verdicts at scale — converting cases that default to "unresolved" into cases resolved correctly.

Prior Art — The Technological Paper

A

Bespoke satellite-evidence report services

OnGeo Intelligence (Poland), Innoter (Russia, since 2014), Grain Data Solutions — all sell court-ready satellite/aerial imagery reports with certified capture dates and metrical data, prepared by human experts. Real courts already accept this evidentiary category.

Limitation: order-a-report services. Days-to-weeks turnaround, no self-serve tool, no automatic multi-source fusion across decades, no AI-generated narrative.

B

Advocacy / human-rights spatial investigation

Forensic Architecture (Goldsmiths, University of London) — methodologically the closest match to HaMuzim's fusion approach anywhere found: multi-source, provenance-conscious, presentation-grade. Kerem Navot / Bimkom already compare historical aerial photos to document West Bank cultivation back to 1980.

Limitation: non-commercial, project-based investigations. Mission is public accountability, not a repeatable product for an individual applicant's case.

C

Government-run internal analysis

The Israel Civil Administration already runs historical-aerial-cultivation analysis internally to classify uncultivated/unregistered land as state land — the exact evidentiary method HaMuzim proposes, already in institutional use.

Limitation: one-sided. It answers the state's question, not the claimant's — HaMuzim arms the other side of a method the state already runs.

D

Emerging research, not yet shipped

2025-era academic and legal writing (Opinio Juris, "From Space to the Courtroom"; a University of Strathclyde paper on transformer models classifying satellite imagery for legal analysis) signals the AI+satellite+legal intersection is a live research question right now.

Limitation: research, not product. Nobody has shipped the productized version.

The Gap

Nobody has shipped a self-serve tool that (1) fuses aerial + satellite + document sources across decades automatically, (2) writes an AI evidentiary narrative, and (3) exports a court-ready exhibit in minutes instead of weeks. Demand for the underlying evidentiary category is proven — the automation + speed + self-serve combination is still open. The real risk isn't "someone already built it" — it's whether courts/committees accept an AI-generated summary with the same evidentiary weight as a named expert's signed report.
