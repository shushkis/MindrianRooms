# Dual-Use Market Definition

"Dual-use" here means: the same core capability (automated historical
aerial-photo cultivation detection with provenance-traced confidence
scoring) serves both a government/administrative buyer and a private
civilian buyer, through opposite commercial paths.

## B. Recommended Initial Market Definition Statement

**Revised 2026-07-21 to reflect the Israel-wide expansion** (see
`israel-wide-market-expansion-2026-07.md`) -- the market is not bounded
to one committee's caseload, it's bounded to one *evidentiary question*
that recurs across multiple distinct Israeli land-law contexts:

> HaMuzim/GroundTruth is a dual-use geospatial-evidentiary platform for
> resolving historical-cultivation land disputes across Israel and the
> territories it administers under Ottoman-derived land law. The
> platform answers one recurring evidentiary question -- can a claimant
> or the state prove or disprove continuous historical cultivation of a
> parcel -- that currently determines outcomes in at least two distinct,
> independently-sized contexts: (1) first-registration disputes in
> Judea and Samaria under Ottoman Land Code Article 78 (miri/waqf land,
> 10-year cultivation), where the state has committed ₪244.1M
> (2026-2030) to a new registration authority; and (2) Bedouin
> land-ownership claims in the Negev under Article 103 (mewat land),
> where ~12,000 claimants dispute ~570,000 dunams and a live 2026
> government pilot is actively compensating or swapping claims at up to
> 25,000 ₪/dunam. Two segments within each context -- government/
> administrative buyers (Civil Administration, רמ"י, the property
> custodian) and private claimants/their law firms -- are served by the
> same core capability (automated historical aerial-photo cultivation
> detection with provenance-traced confidence scoring), sold through
> opposite commercial paths (B2G relationship sales vs. B2B via law
> firms). The Negev segment is likely a *later-stage* market (claims are
> decades-old and the 2026 pilot is framed as closing them out, not
> opening new ones) -- Judea and Samaria remains the near-term focus;
> the Negev finding matters as evidence the underlying capability
> generalizes across Israeli land law, not as an immediate second GTM
> target.
