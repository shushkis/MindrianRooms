# Closing the Two Open Gaps

## Gap 1: Avi's Willingness-to-Pay (20,000-50,000 NIS) -- Partially Corroborated

Independent search on Israeli land-dispute legal costs found: for
"especially difficult cases on registration of real estate rights," fees
run **30,000-100,000 NIS**. This is a *general legal fee* for hard
registration cases, not a quote for an evidentiary-reconstruction report
specifically -- not the same service, so treat it as a bracket, not a
match.

**Read:** the room's existing 20,000-50,000 NIS figure sits at the
low-to-middle end of what claimants already demonstrably pay for hard
registration cases. That's the right direction for a sanity check --
it suggests the figure is conservative, not inflated. It does not
independently prove willingness-to-pay for *this specific* service; only
a real claimant/law-firm conversation closes that fully.

Sources: [Israeli Lawyer Price List](https://israelcrossborderfamilylaw.com/israeli-lawyer-price-list-a-2026-guide-to-legal-fees-costs/), [How much do Israel's lawyers charge?](https://pz-law.co.il/en/blog-en/how-much-do-israeli-lawyers-charge/)

## Gap 2: Independent TAM -- Still Not Closeable by Web Search

Tried three angles to get a case-volume or parcel-count number:

1. Objections-committee / property-custodian office caseload (cases/year) --
   **not found**. No published statistics.
2. Average agricultural parcel size in Area C (needed to convert the
   LTSA's 290,000-dunam five-year target into a case count) -- **not
   found**. PCBS and B'Tselem data give area totals (Palestinians farm
   ~326,000 of ~1.8M accessible dunams in Area C) but not a per-parcel
   average.
3. Private-claimant litigation volume -- **not found** independently of
   Mordi's own domain estimate.

This is now the *third* research pass to hit the same wall (the earlier
market-analyst and competitive-refresh passes hit it on tender data and
procurement data respectively). The pattern is consistent enough to trust:
**this specific number does not exist in public form.** It requires a
phone call to someone inside ISA, the Civil Administration, or the new
LTSA -- not a better search query.

### What We Can Anchor Instead

The one hard, sourced number that *does* exist: the state itself is
committing **NIS 244.1M (~$79M) over 2026-2030** to this problem. That's
not a proxy for what a software vendor could capture -- it's a ceiling
on how seriously the buyer values the underlying problem, i.e. evidence
the budget-authority conversation (Buyer 1, Yossi) is grounded in a real,
sized commitment, not vague interest. Worth using as *credibility* evidence
in a pitch ("the state just put $79M behind this problem"), not as a TAM
figure -- those are different claims and shouldn't be conflated.

## What This Means for the VC Conversation

Both gaps point the same direction: the numbers that would make this a
clean top-down VC pitch (case volume, independent TAM) **do not exist
publicly** -- they exist only inside relationships Mordi already has.
That's not a weakness to paper over; it's the argument for the bootstrap
sequencing discussed earlier. The MVP conversations with MoJ-adjacent
contacts are the *only* path to the real numbers -- which means doing the
MVP isn't just revenue, it's the TAM research.
