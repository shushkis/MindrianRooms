# Persona Research -- What Actually Checks Out

## Dalia / Yossi (Government Buyers) -- The ~70% Unregistered Figure Is Real, Not an Echo

Orit's interview flagged a real methodological worry: the "70%" unregistered
figure she cited came up *after* Mordi walked her through the Mullins deck,
so it couldn't be ruled out that she was echoing a number she'd just seen
rather than recalling it independently. Checked directly, independent of
that interview:

- Land and Water Settlement Commission (LWSC) data: **62% of West Bank
  land is registered** (i.e. ~38% unregistered) by one measure.
- A separate figure cited across multiple sources: **~70% of the West
  Bank is "completely unregistered."**
- Area-specific: **~58% of Area C is unregistered** -- roughly **1.9
  million dunams**.
- Historically, Jordanian-administration registration (1949-1967) covered
  about 30% of the total area before Israel halted the process in 1968.

**Read:** the exact percentage moves depending on which area/measure you
use, but every independent source converges on "a majority of the West
Bank, and the large majority of Area C, has never completed formal
registration." Orit's number holds up -- not because she echoed the deck,
but because multiple independent sources say something in the same range.
Cite the range (58-70%, area-dependent), not a single point figure, in
anything customer- or investor-facing -- more defensible and more honest.

Sources: [UPI](https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2026/02/16/west-bank-land-registration-advances/2611771256675/), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/2/16/israel-to-restart-land-registration-in-west-bank-what-that-means), [Times of Israel](https://www.timesofisrael.com/cabinet-oks-new-west-bank-land-registration-process-critics-decry-de-facto-annexation/), [Peace Now](https://peacenow.org.il/en/government-decision-land-registration)

## Orit-type -- The Portugal/Italy Heir-Search Analogy Partially Checks Out

Orit raised this as speculation, not confirmed knowledge -- worth exactly
that level of confidence, no more:

- **Italy: confirmed, real, and commercially active.** The "1 Euro Houses"
  program (30+ municipalities, started in Sicily, expanded nationally) is
  real -- but its actual bottleneck is exactly the multi-heir fragmentation
  problem HaMuzim's core capability addresses in spirit: many abandoned
  properties are split among heirs who each own a fraction (a room, a
  balcony), and nothing can be sold without consent from all of them.
  Commercial heir-search services already exist (e.g. ItalianLaw.net,
  MyItalianFamily) that reconstruct ownership chains from historical
  registry records back to the late 1800s -- structurally the same job as
  HaMuzim's chain-of-title reconstruction, different domain.
- **Portugal: not independently confirmed at the same level.** Found a
  real government service for buying tax-seized/state-abandoned assets and
  a heir-habilitation (habilitação de herdeiros) legal process, but no
  specific "custodian proactively searches for heirs" program matching
  Orit's description. Treat her Portugal reference as unconfirmed until
  checked further -- the Italy half of the analogy is solid enough to use,
  the Portugal half isn't yet.

**Why this matters beyond Orit's specific persona:** it's independent
evidence that "reconstruct fragmented/lost ownership history from
historical records" is a recognized, monetizable job in at least one other
country's real estate market -- not a HaMuzim-specific invention. Worth a
line in the platform-ambition case (`data-inventory.md`, `find-analogies`
methodology), not just a footnote on one persona.

Sources: [The Portugal News](https://www.theportugalnews.com/news/2024-04-03/homes-for-sale-for-1/87541), [1eurohouses.com](https://1eurohouses.com/tag/abandoned-houses/), [ItalianLaw.net](https://italianlaw.net/resources-italy-succession-law/find-italian-unclaimed-assets/), [MyItalianFamily](https://www.myitalianfamily.com/resources/finding-family-property-italy)
