# Israel-Wide Expansion -- Negev Bedouin Land Claims

## Why This Matters

Every prior pass treated "Israel-wide" as an unresearched, unsized
expansion option. It isn't unresearched anymore -- there's an active,
decades-running, currently-live (2026) parallel land-claims market
inside sovereign Israel, in the Negev, that:

1. Uses the **same core evidentiary need** HaMuzim already solves
   (proving historical continuous cultivation to establish a land
   claim under Ottoman-derived land law)
2. **Has published numbers** -- exactly what the Judea & Samaria case
   volume does not have, after three failed research passes
3. Has an **active government program processing claims right now**,
   in 5 named settlements, in 2026

## The Legal Distinction (Do Not Conflate)

The West Bank/Area C mechanism this room has been building around is
**Ottoman Land Code Article 78** (miri/waqf land, 10 years' continuous
cultivation -> possession right -- verified this session against
[Wikipedia -- Ottoman Land Code of 1858](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Land_Code_of_1858)).

The Negev Bedouin claims mechanism is **Article 103** of the same 1858
Code -- governing *mewat* ("dead"/unclaimed) land, where cultivation
without Ottoman permission could still establish a registration claim.
Different article, different land category, same underlying
evidentiary question: **can the claimant show historical continuous
cultivation?** That's the point that matters for HaMuzim -- Module 3's
capability (automated aerial-photo time-series cultivation detection
with confidence scoring) is not tied to Article 78 specifically. It
answers the cultivation-evidence question regardless of which Ottoman
provision a given claim proceeds under. That makes the platform's core
capability more horizontally applicable than previously scoped, not
less.

## Political Framing Caveat (Same Discipline Already Applied to the West Bank Numbers)

As with Peace Now/Al Jazeera vs. neutral wire coverage on the Judea &
Samaria LTSA story, the Negev sources split along a similar line:
Dukium.org (Negev Coexistence Forum) frames this from a Bedouin-rights
advocacy position; Regavim (a pro-settlement-policy NGO, source found
but not relied on for numbers here) frames it from the opposite
direction. The dunam/claimant *counts* above come from multiple
converging sources including Israeli financial press (Calcalist,
Israel Hayom), which reduces (does not eliminate) the framing risk on
the numbers themselves. The *characterization* of the process ("land
theft" vs. "regularization") is contested exactly the way the West
Bank framing already is -- treat as a fact to plan around, not resolve.

## What This Changes

**Row 4 (Geography options) in `dual-use-market-definition-2026-07.md`
is no longer "unresearched" for the Israel-wide/medium scope.** The
Negev gives a second, *larger and better-sourced* case-volume anchor
(~12,000 claimants, ~570,000 disputed dunams, live 2026 government
program with real budgets) than the West Bank segment currently has at
all. This does not replace the Judea & Samaria thesis -- it's a
second, independently-sized market using the same core technology,
which is a stronger diversification story than "one unsized market,"
not a pivot away from it.

## Open Questions This Raises

- Is the Negev program's claims-processing body (likely רמ"י directly,
  not the Civil Administration) a *third* distinct buyer, with its own
  procurement path -- separate from Buyers 1 and 2 already mapped?
- Does the compensation-vs-land-swap mechanism change what "winning
  the evidentiary question" is worth to a claimant (a fixed ₪/dunam
  payout is a much easier ROI calculation for HaMuzim's private-segment
  pitch than the West Bank's open-ended "land value" framing)?
- The Negev claims are decades-old and largely already adjudicated or
  in late-stage settlement (the 2026 pilot is explicitly framed as
  closing out remaining claims) -- is this a shrinking, not growing,
  market? Worth checking the pilot's intended completion timeline
  before treating it as a long-term expansion target.
- Should this be pursued now, or parked as "second market, prove the
  first one first" -- given the room's own standing bias toward
  bootstrap sequencing (`tam-pricing-gap-closure-2026-07.md`)?
