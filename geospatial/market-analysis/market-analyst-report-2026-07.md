# Market Analyst Report

## The Headline Finding, First

**On February 15-16, 2026, the Israeli cabinet approved a new, funded,
staffed process to register West Bank land for the first time since
1968.** This did not exist when the room's earlier research passes were
done. It changes the market picture more than anything else in this
report, in both directions -- read the whole section before deciding what
it means for the venture, not just the budget number.

### The Concrete Facts

- **New institution:** a "Land Title Settlement Administration" is being
  created inside Israel's Ministry of Justice (under the existing Israel
  Land Registration and Settlement of Rights Authority), moving
  registration authority *out of* the military-run Civil Administration and
  into a civilian body.
- **Budget:** NIS 244.1 million (~$79M) allocated for 2026-2030.
- **Headcount:** 35 new positions across the Justice Ministry, Survey of
  Israel (מפ"י -- already a data source this room relies on), the Defense
  Ministry, and the Civil Administration.
- **Scope:** Area C is ~3.3 million dunams, of which ~1.9 million
  (roughly 58%) is unregistered. The stated target is registering **15% of
  unregistered land within five years** (~290,000 dunams) -- not the whole
  backlog.
- **Timeline:** establishing the registration mechanism itself is expected
  to take ~18 months; each individual plot is expected to take "at least a
  year and a half, if not more." At current ambition, clearing the entire
  backlog would take on the order of decades, not years.
- **Process:** per multiple sources (Times of Israel, Peace Now), the
  burden of proof falls on claimants -- land defaults to state registration
  where ownership isn't proven.

Sources: [UPI](https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2026/02/16/west-bank-land-registration-advances/2611771256675/), [Times of Israel](https://www.timesofisrael.com/cabinet-oks-new-west-bank-land-registration-process-critics-decry-de-facto-annexation/), [Peace Now](https://peacenow.org.il/en/government-decision-land-registration), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/2/16/israel-to-restart-land-registration-in-west-bank-what-that-means), [FMEP](https://fmep.org/resource/settlement-annexation-report-february-20-2026/)

### Read #1: This Is About as Good a Buyer-Timing Signal as a Venture Can Ask For

A brand-new government body, freshly funded, freshly staffed, explicitly
tasked with exactly the problem this venture is built around -- standing
itself up right now, with an 18-month mechanism-design window that (per
public reporting) hasn't finished yet. That is not a hypothetical future
buyer. `Yossi` and `Dalia` (the room's own personas) may need a third,
sharper sibling: **the Land Title Settlement Administration itself**,
which didn't exist as a distinct addressable institution before this
February. Worth a direct line to whoever staffs it, not just the existing
ISA/Civil Administration contacts -- this is a new door, not just a
bigger version of an old one.

### Read #2: This Is Also a Live Political and Reputational Fact, Not Just a TAM Expansion

Peace Now and Al Jazeera characterize this process as enabling "large-
scale dispossession... of up to 83% of Area C," because the burden of
proof is described as "nearly impossible" for Palestinian claimants to
meet under current conditions. This is a genuinely contested public
narrative, not a fringe take -- it's the framing from Peace Now, Al
Jazeera, and referenced in Arab Center DC's "Annexation Through Law"
analysis. Whatever the team's own view, any investor conversation, hire,
or customer relationship touching this specific administration will run
into this framing. That's a fact to plan around, not an opinion to have.

**Where the room's own architecture is a genuine (not just convenient)
answer:** the standing decision that the system is descriptive, not
generative -- it presents evidence with confidence levels and provenance,
it does not decide outcomes -- was made for legal-defensibility reasons
(`initial-exploration.md`), not PR reasons. But it has a second effect
worth naming honestly: the same tool that helps a government custodian
confirm a default-to-state outcome could, with identical architecture,
help a private claimant (`Avi`, buyer segment 3) meet a burden of proof
that critics say is "nearly impossible" today, by reconstructing genuine
historical cultivation evidence from public archives. Which side actually
becomes the paying customer first is a real strategic choice this
finding puts back on the table, not a detail -- it's worth a deliberate
conversation with Mordi, not a default assumption either way.

### Read #3: The Timeline Is Long, and That's Useful Information, Not Bad News

"15% in five years," "18 months just to build the mechanism," "a year and
a half per plot" -- this is not a market that closes fast. That cuts
against urgency-based sales pitches ("act now, huge TAM") and toward a
different, more honest pitch: *this is a multi-decade problem the state
has only just begun funding a fraction of a fix for -- exactly the kind of
long, structurally-guaranteed-to-recur demand a platform, not a one-off
project, should be built for.*

## Adjacent Market Signal: Satellite-Evidence-as-a-Service Is a Live, Funded Category

Confirmed again this pass (no material change from the room's existing
`competitive-analysis/`): OnGeo Intelligence and similar bespoke-report
providers remain the closest real analogs, still order-per-report, not
self-serve. Separately, the broader satellite-imagery/earth-observation
startup category is well-funded generally (Planet ~$1B raised, BlackSky
~$440M, 18 startups raising in the last 12 months per one tracker) -- but
none of that funding is going to legal-evidentiary fusion specifically.
The infrastructure layer (raw imagery access) is well-capitalized; the
"turn imagery into a court-ready, provenance-chained exhibit" layer
remains open. No change to the room's existing "the gap holds" verdict.

## Adjacent Market Signal: Italy's Fragmented-Ownership Property Market

See `persona-research-2026-07.md` for detail -- Italy's "1 Euro Houses"
program and its associated heir-search/title-reconstruction service
industry (ItalianLaw.net, MyItalianFamily) is real, active, and
structurally the same job (reconstruct fragmented/lost chain-of-title from
historical records) applied to a different failure mode (multi-heir
fragmentation vs. unregistered first-title). Worth one line in the
platform-ambition narrative as independent evidence the underlying job
generalizes past this venture's specific geography -- not worth building
toward yet.
