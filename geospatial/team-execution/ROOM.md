Two founders with complementary and non-obvious strengths: a sensor fusion engineer with autonomous systems experience and a combat-tested lawyer with deep domain expertise and existing customer relationships.

## Team As Filed

**Elli** -- Software architect, sensor fusion and autonomous vehicles (11+ years). Israeli Air Force test center veteran. Electronics practical engineer + BSc Computer Science + MSc Bioengineering. Technical lead and product architect.

**Mordi** -- Lawyer + LLM. Land dispute resolution specialist using historical aerial photos and archival documents. Israeli Army (special forces). First distribution channel -- his firm is the design partner and the first customer signal.

## Unfair Advantages
- Mordi is not just a customer, he IS the domain. He has already validated the concept internally. His colleagues confirmed value. That is pre-product market signal.
- Elli's sensor fusion background maps directly to the hardest technical problems: correspondence identification across historical imagery, multi-source registration, georeferencing under degraded conditions.

## Open Questions

- What is Mordi's capacity as a design partner vs. as a co-founder? Is he full-time, part-time, or advisory?
- What technical gaps exist? GIS-specific tooling expertise, legal data standards (court exhibit formats by jurisdiction), front-end for non-technical users?
- Who is missing from the team -- a GIS domain expert, a business development person, a second engineer?
