# Prep: Defining Mordi's Role

## The Contradiction Worth Naming First

`team-execution/ROOM.md` opens with "two founders with complementary and
non-obvious strengths," then two lines later describes Mordi's firm as
"the design partner and the first customer signal" -- customer language,
not founder language. Those are two different structures:

- If Mordi (the person) is a co-founder, his law firm is likely a
  *customer* or *channel*, and having a founder's own firm be the design
  partner creates a real (manageable, but real) conflict-of-interest
  question worth naming out loud, not glossing over: is the product being
  shaped by market-wide demand or by one founder's own book of business?
- If Mordi's firm is the actual design-partner/customer entity, Mordi
  personally may be better modeled as an advisor/BD lead with equity tied
  to the relationships and validation he brings -- not full co-founder
  economics.

Neither framing is wrong. But the room currently asserts both at once,
and the equity conversation depends entirely on which one is true.

## Questions to Bring to Mordi (not to decide alone)

1. Time commitment: full-time, part-time, or advisory-only going forward?
   He's an active practicing lawyer -- this has a real answer, not a
   hypothetical one.
2. Is his firm a customer of HaMuzim, a co-founding entity, or both --
   and if both, how is that conflict handled (market-rate customer,
   most-favored-customer terms, or free access as founder comp)?
3. What decision rights does he want -- product direction, hiring,
   fundraising -- versus what he's content to leave to Elli as
   technical/product lead?
4. Vesting: standard 4-year vest with a 1-year cliff regardless of which
   structure is chosen, so the answer isn't locked in permanently on day
   one.

## Rough Shape of the Two Realistic Structures (reference, not a recommendation)

- **Co-founder:** meaningful equity (commonly 30-50% range for a
  domain-expert-plus-channel co-founder, adjusted for time commitment),
  real decision rights, standard vesting.
- **Design partner / advisor + BD lead:** smaller equity (commonly under
  5%, often 0.5-2% for advisors, more if actively closing deals),
  vesting, no formal governance role, his firm treated explicitly as a
  paying customer under normal commercial terms.
