Two distinct legal dimensions: the IP around the platform methodology, and the jurisdiction-specific legal standards that govern what counts as admissible evidence in land disputes.

## Open Questions

- Is the fusion methodology (decision-level provenance chain architecture) novel enough to protect? Or is the moat operational (data + workflow) rather than IP-based?
- What are the admissibility standards for geospatial evidence in Israeli courts? What format, what chain of custody documentation, what expert certification is required?
- If the platform expands beyond Israel, which jurisdictions have similar dispute structures and what are their evidence standards? EU land registry systems differ significantly from common law systems.
- What entity structure makes sense -- Israeli company, dual-registration, or incorporated elsewhere from day one?
