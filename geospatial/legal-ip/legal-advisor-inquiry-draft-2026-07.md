## Context

Follow-up to `conflict-of-interest-mordi-2026-07.md`. Mordi and Larry worked
through the sequencing question on 2026-07-20: rather than testing appetite
with his ministry's director-general first (risky -- the approach itself,
not just a resulting deal, is the exposure), the agreed path is to open a
low-commitment, low-disclosure inquiry with the ministry's legal advisor
first, before any commercial contact and before the founders' agreement /
incorporation is finalized.

**Key points established this session:**

- General תקשי"ר principle: a permit is technically required even for
  *unpaid* private occupation, not just paid work -- the rationale is
  attention/conflict, not compensation.
- Precedent (תע"א senior executive / startup case): disclosure happened
  **before incorporation**, not after commercial activity began. The
  eventual arrangement allowed passive investment only, no active role --
  not an outright ban. This is the realistic worst case, not "the whole
  project is blocked."
- ~6,007 state employees currently hold valid private-work permits --
  a flat refusal is the exception, not the norm.
- A different public body Mordi doesn't work for (e.g. רמ"י) as the
  eventual customer materially eases the core conflict-of-interest risk,
  though it does not eliminate the general permit requirement (42.1).
  This is why the draft below asks about that distinction explicitly
  rather than assuming it.
- חל"ת (unpaid leave) does **not** exempt Mordi from the permit
  requirement -- the employment relationship continues during חל"ת, per
  Takshir section 42.401(ב) (private work is not a vested right). It may
  make a permit easier to obtain in practice, but it is not a shortcut
  around disclosure.
- Recommended trigger point for updating the founders' equity/role split
  in `mordi-role-decision-2026-07.md`: **after** the legal advisor's
  answer is in hand, not before -- since the answer may reshape whether
  Mordi can hold an active operating role vs. passive-investor-only.
