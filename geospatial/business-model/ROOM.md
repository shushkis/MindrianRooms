The platform ambition and the wedge go-to-market need different business model thinking. The legal application may justify per-case pricing; the platform layer suggests SaaS or API licensing.

## Open Questions

- Per-case or subscription? Mordi's case intake filter (pre-decision scan) suggests a low-cost, high-volume entry product separate from the full dispute support package.
- Is this sold to lawyers directly, to law firms as practice tooling, or to government cadastral agencies as infrastructure?
- What is the platform licensing model when other developers or firms build applications on top? API pricing, white-label, revenue share?
- Who is the first check-writer -- Mordi's firm as design partner, or an outside paying customer from day one?
