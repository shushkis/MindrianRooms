# Competitive Landscape Refresh -- 2026-07

## Verdict: No Material Change

Searched specifically for new entrants in "AI + satellite imagery + legal
evidence + land disputes" and for any technology angle on the new Land
Title Settlement Administration (see `market-analyst-report-2026-07.md`).
Found nothing that changes the room's standing competitive read:

- **OnGeo Intelligence** remains the closest real analog -- still a
  bespoke, order-per-report service, not self-serve, not automated fusion
  across sources. Consistent with the existing `orhitec-review.md` /
  `competitive-landscape.md` framing.
- **No procurement/tender information found** for the new Land Title
  Settlement Administration -- either too new to have surfaced publicly,
  or not published in a place general web search reaches. This is a real
  gap this research pass could not close; direct inquiry or watching the
  Ministry of Justice's tender portal is the only way to close it.
- **The broader satellite-imagery/earth-observation startup category is
  well-funded** (Planet ~$1B raised, BlackSky ~$440M, 18 startups raising
  in the last 12 months per one industry tracker) -- but this capital is
  going to imagery infrastructure and general-purpose analytics, not to
  legal-evidentiary fusion specifically. This is the same read the room
  already had; more capital in the adjacent layer doesn't close the gap
  this venture targets, it just means the raw-imagery layer underneath
  keeps getting cheaper and better, which if anything helps this venture's
  input costs rather than threatens its position.

## What Would Actually Change This Verdict

Any of the following would be worth an immediate follow-up, not routine
monitoring:
1. A published tender from the new Land Title Settlement Administration
   naming a technology vendor or RFP scope.
2. Any of the internally-self-built tools mentioned in customer interviews
   (Orit's own Gemini assistant, the colleague inside the Chief Appraiser's
   office building something similar -- see
   `orit-district-appraiser-meeting.md`) getting formalized, funded, or
   rolled out beyond personal use.
3. OnGeo Intelligence, Innoter, or a similar bespoke-report provider
   launching a self-serve or API product -- would close the automation gap
   the room's whole thesis rests on.

None of these have happened yet, per this pass.
