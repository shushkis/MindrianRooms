# Meeting: Yonatan Sagir -- IRIS Program Debrief

## What Was Validated

Yonatan confirmed the geospatial path over IFF. His reasoning:
- IFF has unsolved liability problem: who is responsible when the system kills the wrong person?
- Geospatial platform has dual-use by definition -- not locked inside defense procurement
- Business potential is not bounded by a single ministry budget

## Key Reframes From Yonatan

### The Breezometer Moment
Breezometer built an air quality sensor. The sensor was the entry point.
The data layer they accumulated became the real product.
Same structure here: legal disputes are the receptor.
The historical spatial intelligence layer is the product.

### The Temporal Arc
Past -> Present: reconstruct historical evidence (Mordi's current job)
Present -> Future: predict what will happen to this land
  - Fire risk (soil dryness trends)
  - Agricultural potential (sun exposure, soil composition)
  - Pipeline routing (where infrastructure runs underground)
  - Development potential (zoning, density, access)
The engine is identical. Output packaging changes per buyer.

### Open Data as Strategic Foundation
Build the base layer from public sources only:
  - Public orthophotos (Israel Survey Institute archives)
  - Historical aerial photography (public domain)
  - Court decisions and land records (public filings)
  - OSINT (drone footage, satellite imagery, social media geo-tagged content)

When government agencies and utilities want insight, they add private data
on top of your base layer. You become infrastructure they plug into.
"You're the socket. They're the plug."

### Infrastructure, Not Solution
Yonatan pushed hard on this: do not position as a product for one use case.
"Instead of being a solution to a problem, be a platform of infrastructure
for many different solutions."
Tactical entry: Mordi's legal disputes.
Strategic goal: the base layer that any spatial-temporal decision maker needs.

## The Domain Name

Yonatan corrected "real estate" as the domain.
"Real estate is a use case. The domain is geospatial intelligence --
the ability to draw conclusions from open information about a geographic
area for purpose X."
Mordi's summary: "Data Geography."

## The Proof of Concept Yonatan Named

"What we're doing with Larry right now is essentially what you want to
do with land data -- aggregate multi-source information and draw
conclusions from it. We just proved it's possible."

He mentioned he works with people who have deep interest in:
- Energy (where to route pipelines)
- Geography and land use
- Fire management (California fires -- aggregate drone footage in real time)
Active demand exists. Not hypothetical.
