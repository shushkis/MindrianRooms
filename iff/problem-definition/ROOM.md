Combat IFF for dismounted infantry is a decades-old problem with no satisfactory solution. Current methods -- operational briefings, eye contact, and static fire zones -- offload identification entirely onto human cognition under stress. These collapse precisely when they are needed most: kinetic contact, low visibility, complex terrain.

Starter questions:
1. What are the specific conditions (time of day, terrain type, unit size, mission type) under which friendly fire incidents are most likely to occur in your experience?
2. The current satisfaction score for IFF methods among dismounted soldiers appears to be ~2/10. What would a 7/10 solution look like in practice -- not technologically, but behaviorally?
3. Is the core failure a detection problem (can't identify the person) or a communication problem (identification doesn't propagate fast enough to the shooter)?
