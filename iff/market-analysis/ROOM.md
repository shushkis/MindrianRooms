Defense tech market analysis is structurally different from commercial. The buyer (procurement) is not the user (soldier). Market size calculations must account for platform counts, upgrade cycles, and program-of-record dynamics, not just total addressable users.

Starter questions:
1. Who actually signs the contract -- brigade-level commanders, SOCOM, NATO procurement? Understanding the buyer-user split is critical before any go-to-market work.
2. What is the Israeli defense procurement path for a dual-use technology with IFF applications? (Your legal background is an asset here.)
3. Are there allied forces (US, UK, NATO) whose procurement cycles are more accessible than domestic Israeli ones as a first commercial beachhead?
