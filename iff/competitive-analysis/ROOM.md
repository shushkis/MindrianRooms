The IFF field has seen billions in investment since WWII. The question is not whether solutions exist -- they do. The question is why they keep failing in dismounted infantry scenarios specifically. Understanding what has been tried, and why it failed at the unit level, is the core competitive intelligence work.

Starter questions:
1. What existing IFF systems have you encountered or been briefed on in your combat deployments? What were their actual failure modes in the field (not in the spec sheet)?
2. The BCIS (Battlefield Combat Identification System), Duke systems, and various RF-based IFF transponders have all had field adoption problems. What is the common thread in why soldiers reject or work around electronic IFF systems?
3. Is your solution differentiated by technology (does something technically new), by form factor (existing technology in a more usable package), or by system design (a different operational concept entirely)?
