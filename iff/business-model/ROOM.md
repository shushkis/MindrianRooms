Defense tech business models are not like SaaS. Revenue comes in large, slow, irregular tranches. The path to first dollar is measured in years, not months. Unit economics must account for procurement cycles, certifications, and the cost of maintaining a cleared sales team.

Starter questions:
1. Is this a hardware product, a software layer on existing hardware, or a dual-use platform that sells into commercial robotics AND defense? The answer determines everything about your capital requirements and timeline.
2. What is your theory of how you get to first contract -- OTA (Other Transaction Authority), SBIR/STTR, direct procurement, or a prime contractor partnership?
3. Who are the Israeli defense tech companies (e.g., Elbit, Rafael, IAI) that might be partners vs. competitors in this space? The distinction matters for your go-to-market.
