# Two Candidate Problems -- Initial Conversation

## Candidate A: Pediatric Epilepsy & De-Novo Genomic Disorders

Elli's oldest child has epilepsy and a developmental disorder caused by a de-novo genome defect. This is not a domain of casual interest -- it is a lived problem with daily stakes.

Elli's background intersects this domain unusually well:
- MSc in Bioengineering (thesis on attention and perception via VR)
- 11 years in sensor fusion and computer vision
- Air Force sensor selection experience

The technical question: can sensor fusion, computer vision, or genomic data analysis create a meaningful intervention -- diagnostic, monitoring, or otherwise -- for this population?

## Candidate B: Geospatial Legal Intelligence

Mordi works as a lawyer resolving disputed land boundaries using old aerial photographs and historical documents. The workflow is manual, slow, and high-stakes (legal outcomes depend on it).

Elli's computer vision stack applies directly to multi-source aerial photo analysis. Mordi provides domain access and legal validation ground truth.

The technical question: can the workflow be automated or significantly accelerated using AI-assisted image analysis and document parsing?

## Larry's Framing

These are two different companies. The decision is not about capability -- both are technically feasible given the team profile. It is about which problem is impossible to walk away from.

Elli named his son's condition before anything else. That is signal.
